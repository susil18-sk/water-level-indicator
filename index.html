Water Level Indicator System

Efficient Water Monitoring Solution

Project Description

The Water Level Indicator using BJT is a circuit designed to detect and indicate water levels in a tank using Bipolar Junction Transistors (BJT). When water reaches different probe levels, it allows current to flow into the base of the transistor, switching it ON and lighting the corresponding LED. This system helps prevent water overflow and ensures efficient water management.

Apparatus Required

  • NPN Transistors (BC547)
  • LEDs
  • Resistors (220Ω – 1kΩ)
  • Connecting Wires
  • DC Power Supply (5V–9V)
  • Water Tank with Probes
  • Breadboard

Apparatus Specification

  • BJT (BC547): NPN transistor
  • LED: Forward voltage ~2V
  • Resistor: 220Ω – 1kΩ
  • Power Supply: 5V/9V DC

Working Principle

  • Water acts as a conductive medium
  • When water touches a probe, base current flows into the BJT
  • The transistor switches ON
  • The corresponding LED glows indicating the water level

Procedure

  1. Connect emitters of all BJTs to ground
  2. Connect LEDs to collectors through resistors
  3. Insert probes at different heights in the tank
  4. Connect each probe to the base of a transistor
  5. Provide DC power supply
  6. Fill water and observe LEDs glowing at different levels

Connection Method

The emitter of each BJT is connected to ground. The collector is connected to an LED in series with a resistor and then to the positive supply (Vcc). The base of each transistor is connected to probes placed at different levels in the tank, while a common probe is connected to Vcc. When water reaches a probe, it completes the circuit, allowing base current to flow, turning the transistor ON and lighting the LED.

Precautions

  • Avoid short circuits
  • Use proper resistor values
  • Ensure probes are insulated properly
  • Do not use high voltage supply

Applications

  • Household Water Tanks: Prevents overflow and water wastage.
  • Industrial Liquid Storage: Monitors chemical or water levels in tanks.
  • Automotive Fuel Monitoring: Measures fuel levels in vehicles.
  • Smart Cities: Flood warning systems and automated water management.

Benefits

  • Simple and low-cost circuit
  • No microcontroller required
  • Easy to design and implement

Disadvantages

  • Less accurate than digital sensors
  • Probe corrosion over time
  • Not suitable for advanced automation
